About OutbreakWatch

OutbreakWatch is a community-powered early warning system designed to help identify and stop infectious disease outbreaks at the earliest possible stage. Built with low-resource communities in mind, it leverages simple yet effective tools like mobile-friendly apps, Google Forms, and open data visualization to make public health surveillance accessible to all.

Why This Project?

Many public health systems in resource-limited settings face challenges in quickly detecting outbreaks. By empowering health workers and the community to report symptoms through a smartphone-friendly interface, we aim to fill critical gaps in early warning and response.

How It Works

1. Community members or staff submit symptom reports via a simple Google Form.
2. Data is collected securely and stored in a connected Google Sheet.
3. The system monitors trends and displays them in real-time via charts and tables.
4. When suspicious patterns are detected, alert messages are triggered on the platform.

Designed for Simplicity and Scalability

This system is designed to be:

  • Low-cost: Uses free, open-source tools and cloud storage.
  • Offline-friendly: Runs as a Progressive Web App (PWA).
  • Community-focused: Built for use by health staff and volunteers.
  • Adaptable: Easily deployable across regions or institutions.

Our Vision

We believe every community deserves access to timely outbreak alerts, regardless of technical or financial barriers. OutbreakWatch is a step toward closing the gap — using simple technology to empower frontline health workers and protect lives.
